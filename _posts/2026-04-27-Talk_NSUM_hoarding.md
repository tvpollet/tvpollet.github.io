Tomorrow I'm presenting what is very much just an idea for a grant at this stage. It's a short talk about using enumeration methods to help us estimate hidden populations in this case, people who hoard.

In the talk, I present this method from Social Network analysis, which is commonly used and evaluate whether it could be useful to estimate the number of people who hoard (to a degree it impacts their daily lives to a substantial detriment). Curious? Have a look at the [slides](https://tvpollet.github.io/Hoarding_NSUM_talk/Hoarding_NSUM.html#1). Please don't steal my idea. Or if you do, run with it ;). I think it would make for a genuinely useful study -- so if you manage to get it funded all the better. Use arrows to navigate the presentation.
